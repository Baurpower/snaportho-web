Chapters General Principles

Surgical Positioning

Learning Objectives

Identify pressure points, nerve risks, and setup checks for common orthopaedic positions.

Supine Position

The supine position is common for trauma and arthroplasty. Confirm bony prominences are padded, the peroneal nerve is protected, and fluoroscopy access is planned before prep.

Lateral Position

The lateral position requires axillary roll placement, secure pelvic supports, and careful assessment of dependent shoulder and peroneal nerve pressure.

Prone Position

The prone position introduces airway, ocular, abdominal pressure, and peripheral nerve risks. Team-based positioning checks reduce preventable complications.
