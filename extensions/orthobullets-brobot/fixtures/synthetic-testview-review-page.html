Trauma Intertrochanteric Fractures

Review Question - QID 3794

A reverse obliquity intertrochanteric fracture is shown after a ground-level fall. Which fixation is most appropriate?
1 Sliding hip screw 14%
2 Short cephalomedullary nail 39%
3 Long cephalomedullary nail 42%
4 Percutaneous screw fixation 5%
Reverse obliquity intertrochanteric fractures are unstable and are best treated with a cephalomedullary device that resists medialization of the shaft.
