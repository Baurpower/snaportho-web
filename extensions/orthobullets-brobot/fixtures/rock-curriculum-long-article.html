Local Anesthesia

Alex Smith, MD

Updated July 2026

Learning Objectives

  • Describe commonly used local anesthetic agents and duration of action.
  • Recognize signs of local anesthetic systemic toxicity.
  • Apply safe dosing principles in orthopaedic procedures.

Overview

Local anesthetics block voltage-gated sodium channels and prevent nerve impulse propagation. In orthopaedics, they are used for minor procedures, fracture reductions, wound care, and perioperative analgesia.

Choice of agent depends on onset, duration, epinephrine use, patient comorbidities, and the tissue bed being injected.

Dosing and Safety

Dose calculations should account for patient weight and total anesthetic administered from all sources. Toxicity can present with circumoral numbness, tinnitus, altered mental status, seizure, or cardiovascular collapse.

Lipid emulsion therapy is used for severe systemic toxicity along with airway support and seizure control.

Clinical Pearls

  • Aspirate before injection and use incremental dosing.
  • Avoid epinephrine in compromised end-arterial fields when perfusion is uncertain.
  • Buffering can reduce injection pain for selected agents.
