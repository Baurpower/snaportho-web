Compartment Syndrome

Compartment syndrome is a surgical emergency caused by increased pressure within a closed fascial compartment.

Clinical Findings

Pain out of proportion, pain with passive stretch, paresthesias, pallor, pulselessness, and paralysis are classic findings, though pulses may remain present early.

FindingTiming
Pain with passive stretchEarly
ParesthesiasIntermediate
ParalysisLate

Management

Emergent fasciotomy is indicated when clinical suspicion is high and perfusion or neurologic function is threatened.
